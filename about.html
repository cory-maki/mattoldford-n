From Trades to Strategy

A Foundation Built in the Field

Matthew Oldford was born in 1980 in Nova Scotia and was drawn to construction from an early age. After graduating high school and attending Nova Scotia Community College (NSCC), he entered the industry at 22 with a local builder, quickly developing foundational skills in both residential and commercial construction. Those years of hands-on work gave Oldford something rare — a builder's instinct that no classroom can fully replicate.

His early career included five years at a coastal-roofing quoting agency, where he worked his way into a management role. Overseeing residential and commercial roofing projects ranging from $20,000 to $250,000, Matt refined his skills in project management, client communication, and quality control — the same pillars that define his development work today. You can follow his ongoing work on his LinkedIn profile.

The Finance Chapter

Where Construction Met Capital

In 2007, Matthew Oldford made a move that would define his career edge: he returned to NSCC to pursue investment-management accreditation. Completing the Canadian Securities Course (CSC), the Life Licence Qualification Program (LLQP), and related financial training, he transitioned into a five-year role as a financial planner with Scotiabank, followed by work as a mobile mortgage specialist. This wasn't a detour — it was an education that made him a fundamentally better developer.

The ability to evaluate a property as a financial instrument — not just a construction project — is what separates Oldford from most builders in Atlantic Canada. Every renovation budget, every multi-unit pro forma, every project timeline carries that financial discipline. Explore his thinking further on his Medium writing archive, where he shares perspectives on development and investment.

Back to the Build

Foreman Work and the Multi-Unit World

Around 2017, Matt returned fully to the construction world, this time as a foreman with LIUNA. Supervising 10–15 crew members and managing large-scale, multi-unit job sites deepened his understanding of the logistics that determine whether a development succeeds or stalls. Timelines, budgets, subcontractor management, safety protocols — all of it became second nature at scale.

That hands-on leadership experience shapes how Matthew Oldford operates today. He doesn't just commission builds; he understands precisely what's happening at every stage. The transition from renovation contractor to multi-unit developer wasn't a leap — it was a natural progression for someone who had been building toward it for two decades.

Matty's Renos & Beyond

Building a Company, Then a Development Portfolio

In 2018, Oldford launched Matty's Renos, a renovation and design-build company focused on craftsmanship, transparency, and client experience. The company built its reputation on delivering real value — treating every renovation as an investment, not just a construction job. His financial background gave clients something they rarely get: a builder who could also speak honestly about what their project would return. Find project highlights and visual documentation on his Behance portfolio.

Over time, Matty's Renos evolved from residential renovations into larger property investments and multi-unit developments. Today, Matthew is completing a 17-unit building on Prince Albert Road in Halifax — a landmark project in his transition from builder to full-scale developer — and launching two new purpose-built student housing projects in Halifax's South End, responding to the city's growing need for quality rental supply.

Community & Values

Beyond the Build Site

Outside of work, Matthew Oldford is known for his commitment to community and personal wellness. He volunteers with Feed Nova Scotia and supports local food-security initiatives — a reflection of his belief that strong communities and strong buildings go together. He practices yoga, prioritizes fitness, and values family time as anchors in a demanding industry.

He also provides informal mentorship to young tradespeople and aspiring entrepreneurs in the construction sector, sharing lessons from a career that has crossed trades, finance, and development. His journey from apprentice builder to developer reflects the kind of adaptability, integrity, and long-term vision Halifax needs as it grows.
